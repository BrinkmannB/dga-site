Strategic Autonomy Review

Understanding how dependencies influence strategic autonomy.

A structured governance review that helps boards and executives understand how dependencies influence strategic autonomy, decision-making and future freedom of action.

What is a Strategic Autonomy Review?

Most organizations understand the technologies they use. Far fewer understand the dependencies those technologies create.

The Strategic Autonomy Review provides a structured analysis of the dependencies that influence an organization's strategic autonomy, decision-making and future options.

The purpose is not to eliminate dependency. The purpose is to understand it.

Why does it matter?

Dependencies rarely emerge as strategic decisions.

They accumulate through convenience, efficiency, integration and optimization.

Over time these dependencies may influence governance, decision-making and an organization's freedom of action.

Understanding those dynamics is becoming increasingly important for boards and executives.

What does the Review examine?

  • Supply chains and external suppliers
  • Knowledge and technology dependencies
  • Financial dependencies
  • Data and information dependencies
  • Governance and decision-making structures

How does the Review work?

The Review is based on a structured governance analysis.

It examines dependencies, assesses their strategic relevance, evaluates their influence on decision-making and identifies potential vulnerabilities.

The objective is to understand how these factors influence strategic autonomy and future freedom of action.

Expected Output

Each Review results in an Executive Briefing.

The briefing summarizes key observations, strategic implications and potential areas requiring further discussion.

Its purpose is to support informed decision-making at board and executive level.

Executive Briefing

The Executive Briefing translates observations into governance questions.

It helps leaders understand which dependencies matter, where strategic vulnerabilities may exist and what options remain available.
