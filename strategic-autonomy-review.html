Strategic Autonomy Review

Strategic autonomy is shaped by dependencies long before organizations recognize them.

A structured governance review that helps boards and executives understand how dependencies influence strategic autonomy, decision-making and future freedom of action.

Why does it matter?

Most organizations focus on adopting new technologies. Far fewer examine how those choices influence autonomy, decision-making and future freedom of action. Dependencies rarely emerge as strategic decisions. They accumulate through convenience, efficiency, integration and optimization. Over time these dependencies shape what organizations can do, how they make decisions and how much strategic flexibility they retain.

What is a Strategic Autonomy Review?

The Strategic Autonomy Review is a structured governance review that helps organizations understand which dependencies matter, how they influence decision-making and where strategic vulnerabilities may be emerging. The objective is not to eliminate dependency. The objective is to understand its influence on future choices.

What does the Review examine?

Supply Chains

Dependencies on suppliers, ecosystems and external partners.

Technology & Knowledge

Critical technologies, expertise and capabilities outside the organization.

Financial Dependencies

Financial relationships that may influence strategic flexibility.

Data & Information

Dependencies involving data ownership, information access and digital infrastructure.

Governance

How governance structures influence oversight, accountability and control.

How does the Review work?

The Review is based on a structured governance analysis. It examines dependencies, assesses their strategic relevance, evaluates their influence on decision-making and identifies potential governance implications. The outcome is a clearer understanding of how dependencies influence strategic autonomy and future freedom of action.

Expected Output

Each Strategic Autonomy Review results in an Executive Briefing. The briefing summarizes key observations, governance implications and areas requiring further discussion. Its purpose is to support better-informed strategic decision-making.
