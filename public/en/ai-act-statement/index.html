AI Act Statement

Transparency and accountability for AI systems

1. Purpose of this Statement

This AI Act Statement outlines the intended use, functioning, risks and governance measures related to AI systems assessed or supported by Dutch Governance Advisory. It follows the transparency, documentation and accountability obligations of the EU AI Act.

2. System Classification (AI Act)

AI systems are classified based on:

  • Intended purpose and operational context
  • Potential impact on individuals and public values
  • Degree of autonomy and human oversight
  • Risks related to discrimination, safety or fundamental rights
  • Data sources, model type and documentation quality

Classification outcomes may include: Minimal Risk, Limited Risk, High Risk, or Prohibited Practices.

3. Risk & Impact Assessment

All AI systems undergo a structured assessment focusing on:

  • Fundamental rights impact
  • Fairness, bias and discrimination risks
  • Explainability and transparency obligations
  • Human oversight and auditability
  • Data quality, governance and lifecycle management

4. Transparency & Explainability

The system’s logic, data flows, limitations and dependencies are described in clear and accessible terms. This includes:

  • Model assumptions and constraints
  • Sources and quality of training data
  • Performance metrics and known limitations
  • Which tasks are automated vs. human-controlled

5. Responsibilities & Governance

Governance responsibilities are defined across:

  • Accountability and escalation lines
  • Operational monitoring and audit cycles
  • Model updates, retraining and versioning
  • Safeguards for fundamental rights and public values
