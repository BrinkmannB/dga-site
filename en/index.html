How I support public organisations

AI governance is not primarily a technical challenge. It is a question of responsibility, oversight and institutional design.

I support ministries, municipalities, regulators and other public-sector bodies in translating regulatory requirements — including the EU AI Act — into workable governance structures and decision-making processes.

Core services

AI Act Implementation

Guidance on classification, documentation, risk management and compliance obligations under the EU AI Act, with a focus on high-risk AI systems.

Focus: legal interpretation, governance design and practical implementation.

Algorithm Registers & Transparency

Support in designing, improving and maintaining algorithm registers and transparency profiles in line with European and national requirements.

Focus: explainability, accountability and public trust.

AI Governance Frameworks

Development of governance frameworks defining roles, responsibilities, escalation paths and oversight mechanisms for AI use within public organisations.

Focus: decision-making authority and institutional responsibility.

Working approach

My approach is independent, careful and rooted in public-sector realities. No buzzwords, no technical overreach — but clear analysis, realistic choices and governance that works in practice.
